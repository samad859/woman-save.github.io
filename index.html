Self Defense & Tips

Show that you're Aware:

Show the environment that you are aware of what's happening around you. Potential assailants prefer women that seem distracted or preoccupied. If you look assertive and aware, they might never initiate the assault.

Prepare Your body

If you sense danger, immediately go to a starting fighting position. Move your hands close to your chest, place your right foot a bit ahead of the left and tilt your body forward. Your body is now ready for a physical confrontation.

Have a weapon to reach for:

An umbrella, a set of keys or a pen can make all the difference in a moment of crisis. Remember where they are and be prepared to draw them if you feel threatened.

Call for help:

Calling for help may not provide it but it may scare the attacker enough to allow you to escape. Many women get confused and don't know what to scream. Shouting 'fire' for instance, can be better and more attention grabbing than simply yelling 'help'

Know the weak spots in the body:

If you do find yourselves in a physical confrontation, you can scratch, pull, hit or grab, as you aim directly to one of the five weak spots: Eyes, nose, neck, knees and groin!

Don't be scared to fight:

Many women are scared to strike back because they are scared of their assailant's anger, or are afraid to hurt themselves. The punch you give may hurt you, but the pain is minor and if it's enough to allow you to escape - it's well worth it.

Saftey Measures

It’s depressing to see a question like this in the first place. We as a society have evolved in almost every possible way. But when it comes to women’s safety we fail. ‘ FAIL’ is an understatement here, we are not even AWARE. The statistics say it all.When you ask, what measures should be taken for women’s safety? I don’t know how do I articulate since women are harassed everywhere. Women’s safety in the workplace, in the marketplace, at homes, and in any public place are crucial to ensure women’s safety in

START WITH AWARENESS:

Aware people left, right and center. Talk about the issue in public and especially start at your homes. We always teach our girl child to behave in a particular way, we certainly have the do’s and don’ts for them. This conditioning stops the women to speak up when they are harassed sexually. They are ashamed. They fear consequences and so on. Why can't we give our girls more freedom? Why don't teach our boys to treat the girls right? We have evolved so much with time and technology. But when comes to respect our women we fail big time SELF DEFENSE: Yes! you heard it right, Be self-sufficient and take self-defense training so that you can take care of yourselves. This will you to gain confidence and fight for yourselves at any adversity you face.

KNOW YOUR RIGHTS AND THE LAW:

You should know your rights and the laws against sexual harassments. There are strict laws against it.Sexual harassment is the unwelcome sexual behavior, which makes a person feel offended, humiliated, degraded or intimidated. It is non-consensual and unacceptable. When you face any such harassments speak up and file a complaint against the harasser.PEPPER SPRAY: Always carry a pepper Spray wherever you travel. This is one of the most effective ways to tackle the harasser when you don't have anybody to look out for.

TAKE SELF-DEFENSE TRAINING:

It is important to evaluate the goals and practical usefulness of a women’s self-defense program before signing up.Here are two tips: - Avoid martial arts studios** unless you specifically wish to train in the traditional martial arts techniques and are prepared for a long-term commitment. Many women’s self-defense programs teach watered-down martial arts techniques that are complex and unrealistic under the stress of an actual attack. - The self-defense program should include simulated assaults**, with a fully padded instructor in realistic rape and attack scenarios, to allow you to practice what you’ve learned.

YOU HAVE A RIGHT TO FIGHT:

Unfortunately, no matter how diligently we practice awareness and avoidance techniques, we may find ourselves in a physical confrontation. Whether or not you have self-defense training, and no matter what your age or physical condition, it is important to understand that you CAN and SHOULD defend yourself physically. You have both the moral and legal right to do so, even if the attacker is only threatening you and hasn’t struck first. Many women worry that they will anger the attacker and get hurt worse if they defend themselves, but statistics clearly show that your odds of survival are far greater if you do fight back. Aim for the eyes first and the groin second. Remember, though, to use the element of surprise to your advantage — strike quickly, and mean business. You may only get one chance.
